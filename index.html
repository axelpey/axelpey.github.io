Axel Peytavin

I'm currently co-founding a robotics startup in the Y Combinator Fall 2024 batch, where we're working to bring physical life to AI agents. I believe that technology should do more than just automate tasks—it should create meaningful, intuitive interactions between humans and machines. My vision is to build robots that aren't merely useful tools but are intelligent, interactive companions that seamlessly integrate into our daily lives and transform the way we interact with technology.

Previously, I've been deeply involved in research and innovation at places like Stanford University, where I focused on using AI to counter misinformation and enhance our relationship with media. I also contributed to The Ocean Cleanup by applying physics, machine learning, and environmental science to model ocean plastic pollution, and I founded GetAlong, a project aimed at fostering more constructive conversations on news platforms. These experiences have shaped my approach to technology, anchoring my work in a commitment to societal impact and human-centered design.

I am also an avid fan of Isaac Asimov's literature, in particular the concept of Psychohistory and his anticipation of positronic robots.
